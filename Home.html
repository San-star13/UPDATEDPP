Basic introduction to all languages

  • Japenese:-

    • Japanese is a fascinating language with a rich cultural history. It uses three writing systems: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana is a phonetic script used for native Japanese words and grammatical elements.
    Katakana is also phonetic but is primarily used for foreign words, names, and onomatopoeia.
    Kanji consists of characters borrowed from Chinese, each representing a meaning or idea.

  • Korean:-

    Korean uses a unique writing system called Hangul, created in the 15th century. It consists of 14 basic consonants and 10 basic vowels, which combine to form syllables.
    • korean pronunciation can be challenging for beginners. Each character in Hangul represents a sound, and syllables are formed by combining these characters.
    Korean grammar is quite different from English. It typically follows a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) structure. For example, “I eat an apple” would be structured as “I apple eat.”

  • Chinese:-

    • Chinese is part of the Sino-Tibetan language family. The most widely spoken form is Mandarin, but there are many other dialects, including Cantonese, Wu (Shanghainese), and Min (Hokkien/Taiwanese).
    Chinese uses characters, known as hanzi. Each character typically represents a syllable and can be a standalone word or part of a compound. There are thousands of characters, but around 2,000-3,000 are commonly used in daily life.
    Chinese grammar is quite different from English. It generally follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) structure. However, it lacks verb conjugations and plural forms, relying more on context and particles to convey meaning.

    Basic Phrases

  • Japanese

    • Hello: こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
    Thank you: ありがとう (Arigatou)
    Goodbye: さようなら (Sayounara)
    Yes: はい (Hai)
    No: いいえ (Iie)

  • Korean

    • 안녕하세요 (annyeong haseyo) - Hello
    안녕히 가세요 (annyeonghi gaseyo) - Goodbye (when someone is leaving)
    안녕히 계세요 (annyeonghi gyeseyo) - Goodbye (when you are leaving)

  • chinese

    • 你好 (nǐ hǎo) - Hello 早上好 (zǎo shang hǎo) - Good morning
    晚上好 (wǎn shang hǎo) - Good evening
    再见 (zài jiàn) - Goodbye
